Our History

Kenya Anglican Men’s Association (K.A.M.A), is a men’s fellowship in the Anglican Church of Kenya whose aim is to equip men to transform the family, church and society through the gospel of Christ.

The first men’s fellowship in the Anglican Church in Kenya was commonly known as the Father’s Association in the late 1990’s. In the early 2000’s, the Province harmonized the various Diocesan Fathers’ constitutions, and established the current Kenya Anglican Men’s Association (KAMA). The committee came up the KAMA motto which is derived from Joshua 24:15b, “…as for me and my house, we will serve the Lord.”

Ministry in Action

Fellowship and mentorship

To hold regular meetings for prayer, Bible Study and fellowship and to offer Biblical mentorship programs to the young adults.

Spiritual Growth

Bible study, prayer meetings, and spiritual mentorship for women of all ages.

Capacity Building

To organize conferences, seminars, educational trips, exchange visits and other activities for capacity building.

ACK St. Faith's Church is committed to spreading the Gospel, building strong Christian families, and serving the community with love and truth.
